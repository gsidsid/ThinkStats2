### Estimating the impact of negative Yelp ratings using business closures
Siddharth Garimella

After using as much data as possible from the Yelp Academic dataset to derive business lifespans, I generated survival curves for businesses in each Yelp rating tier using Kaplan-Meier estimation, and compared them with each other. Confusingly, the survival curves of the highest and lowest rated businesses were highly similar, contrary to the expected stratification. After some further analysis of the way I had defined rating tiers for clear plotting, I found that the survival curves within each tier were not self-similar, and in fact there were three overall groups of data with distinguishable survival curves. The resulting groupings, however, do not make much sense. Either these groups should be scrutinized further for relationships, or there is something flawed in this analysis.
